Strategy, context & KPIs

Why 2026 is structured around Asset Manager, Studio, and deployable Industry workflows — and what we measure.

Document author: David Ménard Approvers: Henning Linn, Etienne Whittom, Keyang Xiang, Brian McGovern, Simon Nagel

Context

Industry and Authoring Platform workstreams need clear timelines and deliverables for the Industry strategy so leadership can balance resourcing and produce an executable plan.

2026 operational strategy

Unity Industry is executing a platform-centric shift: expanding beyond the Editor to reach more users via Studio and Asset Manager. That shift supports a net-new logo sales motion with a more accessible SKU (noted at $799).

Barriers to adoption

Onboarding

Hard to onboard users: purchasing, seats, permissions, RBAC, and asset sharing — dashboards and flows are misaligned with target personas.

Deployability

Asset Manager is difficult to deploy in private cloud due to architectural complexity and immature release process (example: long enterprise deployment cycles).

Pillar 1 — Fine-grained access control & asset sharing

Problem: RBAC is only at project level; customers cannot control access per asset or share cleanly. Called out as the most requested capability; BMW and Mercedes are waiting in 2026.

Direction: Plastic as storage to leverage file-/asset-level Plastic RBAC.

KPIs

  • Cut median upload time for assets >5GB by 50% vs. 2025 baseline (blocked on AM 2.0 Alpha).
  • <1% transfer failure rate with ≥95% successful resume for interrupted uploads.
  • Stickiness (DAU/MAU) for activated users: [12.4]% → [20]%.
  • [30]% of active projects using custom or scoped permission roles.
  • Shared/reused assets: [14]% → [25]%.

Pillar 2 — Security, VPC & on-prem deployability

Context: ~95% of pipeline needs private cloud or on-prem. Public-cloud features are not always on VPC, with gaps in documentation. Goal: scalable deployment, self-deploy paths, and partner/SI onboarding (BAH, Globant, EPAM, resellers).

Direction: Simplify architecture (including Plastic, reducing dependencies e.g. MongoDB removal), new VPCTL deployment (Go-based) vs. Terraform — collectively AM 2.0.

KPIs

  • Support tickets: average <[1] per customer (current: 0.4).
  • Engineering escalation from support: <[10]%.
  • Public vs. private feature parity lag: [30] days → [1] day.

Pillar 3 — Pipeline automation & Asset Transformer

Value: Industry differentiation is ingesting many CAD formats via Asset Transformer on Pipeline Automation; customer-customizable pipelines drive adoption.

Direction: Continue UPA for VPC/on-prem; new actions in AM and AT apps; respond to UX feedback (e.g. moving pipelines across projects/orgs); implement pricing models from linked materials; update format support (HOOPS, Revit, Solidworks, etc.).

KPIs

  • 5% of Industry-licensed orgs using custom pipelines (current: 0.03%).
  • Industry toolkit activation: [15]% → [30]% (current: 13%).
